    <h2 class="headline">India Dispatches Second Major Consignment of Medical Aid to Fight Ebola in Africa</h2>

    <p class="subdeck">43-tonne relief package containing protective equipment, diagnostics and medicines reaches Africa CDC to strengthen containment efforts in Democratic Republic of Congo.</p>

    <div class="byline"><b>Special Correspondent</b> · New Delhi</div>

    <div class="body">

     

      <div class="newsfig">

        <img src="ebola-aid-cargo-plane.jpg" alt="Medical supplies loaded inside Indian aircraft">

        <figcaption><b>ON BOARD</b> Pallets of critical medical supplies being transported to Africa</figcaption>

      </div>

 

      <p class="lead">In a powerful demonstration of global health solidarity, India has dispatched its second tranche of emergency medical assistance to support African nations battling a fresh Ebola outbreak.</p>

     

      <p>The 43-tonne consignment, which includes personal protective equipment, advanced diagnostic tools, monitoring devices, essential medicines and nutritional supplements, was sent to the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC).</p>

 

      <div class="newsfig">

        <img src="iaf-plane-night-loading.jpg" alt="Indian Air Force aircraft loading aid at night">

        <figcaption><b>NIGHT OPERATIONS</b> Indian aircraft being loaded with medical relief at an Indian airfield</figcaption>

      </div>

 

      <h3>Swift Response to Emerging Crisis</h3>

      <p>The latest shipment follows the first tranche dispatched on May 29. The supplies have been routed through Africa CDC’s Eastern Regional Coordinating Centre in Uganda before being moved to the affected eastern regions of the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), where the Bundibugyo strain of Ebola has been reported.</p>

 

      <p>Africa CDC expressed deep gratitude for India’s timely support, stating that the assistance will significantly bolster ongoing containment operations and help protect vulnerable communities.</p>

 

      <h3>India’s Growing Role in Global Health Diplomacy</h3>

      <p>External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar highlighted India’s commitment to standing with Africa during challenging times. This latest move reinforces India’s position as a reliable first responder in international public health emergencies.</p>

 

      <p>Experts note that India’s rapid response not only provides immediate relief but also strengthens long-term health infrastructure across the African continent through capacity-building support.</p>

 

      <h3>Continued Commitment</h3>

      <p>New Delhi has reiterated that it remains in close coordination with African health authorities and is prepared to send further tranches of medical and logistical support as the situation demands. This effort underscores India’s deep-rooted philosophy of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam — the world is one family.</p>

function downloadAsPDF() {

    let headline = document.querySelector(".headline") ?

                   document.querySelector(".headline").textContent.trim() : "Article";

   

    let cleanTitle = headline

        .replace(/[^a-zA-Z0-9\s-]/g, "")

        .replace(/\s+/g, "-")

        .toLowerCase()

        .substring(0, 60);

   

    const filename = "bharatsamwad-2026-06-02-page2-" + cleanTitle + ".pdf";

 

    const toast = document.createElement("div");

    toast.style.cssText = "position:fixed;bottom:30px;left:50%;transform:translateX(-50%);background:#1a1a1a;color:white;padding:14px 28px;border-radius:6px;z-index:10000;font-size:1rem;";

    toast.textContent = "Opening Print to PDF...";

    document.body.appendChild(toast);

 

    setTimeout(() => {

        toast.remove();

        window.print();

    }, 600);

}

  });

}
