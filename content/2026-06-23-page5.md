LEAD: In a major demonstration of New Delhi’s expanding infrastructure footprint in Central Asia, External Affairs Minister Dr. S. Jaishankar inspected progress at the construction site of the Mongol Refinery Project in Altanshiree, Dornogovi Province. Walking the site alongside Mongolian Foreign Minister Battsetseg Batmunkh and Industry and Mining Minister Gongor Damdinnyam on Tuesday, June 23, 2026, Jaishankar reviewed the engineering milestones of a project that has become a symbol of India's development diplomacy. Funded via a massive USD 1.7 billion soft Line of Credit (LoC) channeled through the Exim Bank of India, the greenfield refinery is officially India’s largest single development partnership asset globally. The high-profile site visit highlights a calculated geopolitical pivot: helping landlocked Mongolia secure energy independence while extending India's reach into a region traditionally dominated by Russia and China.
