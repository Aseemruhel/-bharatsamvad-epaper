LEAD: India is preparing to host the fourth India-Africa Forum Summit this year, signalling a renewed commitment to one of the most significant partnerships in the Global South. Leaders and representatives from across Africa are expected to participate in discussions aimed at expanding cooperation in trade, technology, infrastructure, healthcare and sustainable development.

The summit is being viewed as an important step toward strengthening political coordination and building long-term economic resilience between India and African nations. Officials and diplomats have described the partnership as one based on equality, mutual respect and shared developmental priorities.

## What the agreement covers

According to officials, the framework spans defence technology, joint manufacturing and training. It also envisages collaboration on emerging technologies and supply-chain resilience.

The text of the agreement is expected to be made public in the coming days. Officials said implementation will be carried out in phases.

## What it means

Experts believe the framework will give both countries greater strategic flexibility. Joint manufacturing, in particular, could create new industrial opportunities within India.

## The road ahead

The fourth India-Africa Forum Summit comes at  time of growing geopolitical uncertainty, technological transformation and shifting economic alignments. For both India and African countries, the partnership is increasingly seen as central to building resilience in the Global South.

Diplomats and analysts believe the summit could help accelerate cooperation in energy security, supply chains, manufacturing, innovation and digital connectivity while reinforcing political coordination on global issues.

Overall, the India-Africa partnership is entering a new phase shaped by shared developmental goals, expanding economic ties and a common vision for a more balanced international order. The success of the upcoming summit will depend on how effectively these ambitions are translated into practical and long-term cooperation frameworks.
