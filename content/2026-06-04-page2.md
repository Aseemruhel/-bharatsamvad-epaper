LEAD: The Union government has revised its visa and security clearance framework, removing a long-standing requirement for dual approval in certain categories of foreign nationals. According to officials in the Ministry of Home Affairs, individuals who receive security clearance through the Security Clearance Portal will no longer need a separate Prior Reference Category clearance.

The move is expected to simplify India's visa administration system while maintaining security oversight through a unified digital clearance mechanism.

## What has changed

Under the revised Visa Manual, the earlier requirement for a two-step clearance process has been scrapped for eligible applicants. Previously, certain foreign nationals needed both PRC clearance and SCP-based security approval before attending events in India.

Officials said the updated system removes duplication by recognising SCP clearance as sufficient for visa processing in these cases. The change is part of broader administrative updates aimed at improving efficiency in visa-related services handled by the MHA.

## Understanding PRC and SCP

The Prior Reference Category applies to specific groups of foreign nationals who require additional scrutiny before entering India. This includes citizens from countries such as Afghanistan, Pakistan, Iraq, Sudan and China for select categories, as well as individuals of Pakistani origin and stateless persons. These individuals typically require clearance for participation in events such as conferences, sports competitions and other organised visits.

The Security Clearance Portal, on the other hand, is a centralised digital system used by government ministries and departments to request and process security approvals for foreign nationals attending such events.

## Key administrative updates

Officials said the latest revision to the Visa Manual goes beyond the PRC simplification. It also formalises several operational provisions that were previously not clearly defined. These include clearer rules for visa extensions and conversions, expanded guidelines for visa-related services and the formal inclusion of multiple-entry provisions under the e-Visa system.

According to officials, these updates are intended to reduce ambiguity in visa processing and improve consistency across departments.

## Why this matters

The change is part of India's broader effort to digitise and streamline immigration and visa processes while maintaining strict security screening standards. By consolidating approvals into a single platform, the government aims to reduce processing delays for event-based travel without diluting oversight.

For ministries and departments that regularly host international conferences, sports events and diplomatic engagements, the simplified process could significantly reduce administrative workload and turnaround time.

## Balancing security and efficiency

Officials emphasised that the revision does not reduce security scrutiny but instead integrates it into a single workflow. The SCP remains the central mechanism for evaluating security risk, while visa processing now relies more directly on its outcome.

This approach reflects a wider trend in government services towards digital consolidation, where multiple approval layers are replaced with unified clearance systems.

## The road ahead

With the updated Visa Manual now in effect, implementation will depend on how quickly ministries adapt their internal processes to the revised framework. Further refinements are expected as the SCP system expands its coverage and integrates additional visa categories. Future updates may focus on further automation of visa decisions and tighter integration between security databases and immigration workflows.
