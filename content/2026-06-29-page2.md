LEAD: India has officially reclaimed its position as the world’s fifth-largest stock market by market capitalization, successfully overtaking Taiwan and South Korea just weeks after sliding behind them. According to global exchange data released on Monday, 29 June 2026, India's aggregate equity valuation breached the psychological $5 trillion threshold to land at approximately \*\*$5.05 trillion\*\*. The rapid realignment in Asian high finance underscores a broader structural rotation out of overextended artificial intelligence hardware plays back into resilient, macro-economically stable emerging markets.



The global top four spots continue to be held by the United States, mainland China, Japan, and Hong Kong. However, the battle for the fifth position has seen intense volatility over the last 60 days, acting as a direct barometer for changing global risk appetite and sector rotation.



\## The mechanics of the flip: Tech corrections vs. domestic resilience



The primary catalyst for India's climb back up the ladder is a double-edged dynamic: a sharp cooling-off period in East Asian tech hubs paired with steady, broad-based domestic accumulation in Indian benchmarks.



In May 2026, an unprecedented boom in AI infrastructure and foundry demand propelled Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co (TSMC) and South Korean memory giants like Samsung and SK Hynix to record valuations. That breakneck rally saw global funds rotate heavily into Taipei and Seoul, briefly pushing India down to the seventh spot. However, June brought a significant reversal:



\* \*\*Taiwan’s Market Correction:\*\* Dropped \*\*2.3%\*\* in June as investors aggressively booked profits on stretched technology and hardware valuations, slipping to $4.97 trillion.

\* \*\*South Korea’s Market Slide:\*\* Declined \*\*4.7%\*\* over the same period, bringing its aggregate capitalization down to $4.66 trillion.

\* \*\*India’s Capital Outperformance:\*\* Moved counter-cyclically to the global trend, climbing \*\*2.75%\*\* over the month.

\## The fundamental drivers powering Indian indices



Analysts point to three distinct structural tailwinds that allowed the benchmark BSE Sensex and NSE Nifty 50 to gain 3.8% and 2.8% respectively in dollar terms this month, thoroughly outpacing their global peers.



\### 1. The crude oil decompression



The most significant macro boost came from a sharp decline in international crude oil prices following the resolution of West Asian maritime transit disputes and a resumption of normalized tanker flows through the Strait of Hormuz. Because India relies on imports for over 80% of its oil needs, lower crude prices directly contract the national import bill, suppress domestic inflation indicators, and fortify the current account balance. Historically, Indian equities perform optimally when crude parameters settle comfortably below the $90 per barrel threshold.



\### 2. Attractiveness in valuation multiples



During its peak over the last year, the Nifty 50 traded at a premium price-to-earnings (P/E) multiple of nearly 24 times, triggering caution among value-driven global funds. Strategic consolidations and strong underlying corporate earnings growth have since moderated the Nifty P/E ratio down to a far healthier \*\*18 times\*\*, significantly improving risk-reward ratios for institutional buyers.



\### 3. Return of foreign institutional investors (FIIs)



After pulling a massive amount of capital out of Indian equities early in the year to chase the semiconductor hardware surge in North Asia, FIIs have flipped back to net buyers. Driven by recent proactive measures from the Reserve Bank of India to ease foreign access into domestic debt and equity frameworks, global funds injected over $1 billion into Indian equities over recent consecutive trading sessions.



\## Outlook for the second half of 2026



While Taiwan and South Korea retain structural dominance over the global tech manufacturing pipeline, India's deep, diverse market composition—anchored heavily by banking, financial services, consumer goods, and an expanding domestic manufacturing framework—offers a stabilizing alternative during global tech volatility.



As geopolitical variables ease and global interest rate cycles begin to turn, India's reclaimed $5 trillion footprint establishes a highly formidable floor for the country’s capital markets heading into the latter half of the year.
