India Reclaims Fifth-Largest Equity Market Position, Crosses $5 Trillion Milestone

Strong domestic fundamentals, easing global headwinds and renewed investor confidence propel Indian equities back into the world's top five stock markets.

India has regained its position as the world's fifth-largest equity market, with its aggregate market capitalisation crossing $5.05 trillion, following a strong rally in domestic equities during June. The recovery comes after Indian markets briefly slipped to seventh position earlier this year amid a technology-led surge in Taiwan and South Korea.

The latest rebound reflects improving macroeconomic conditions, softer crude oil prices, renewed foreign institutional investment, and stronger market fundamentals that helped Indian indices outperform several global peers.

Domestic Markets Lead the Recovery

Benchmark indices continued their upward trajectory during June, with the **BSE Sensex** and **NSE Nifty 50** posting gains supported by broad-based buying across banking, financial services, consumer goods and manufacturing sectors.

Analysts noted that India's diversified market composition provided greater resilience compared to technology-heavy East Asian markets, where profit-booking in semiconductor stocks led to moderation in valuations.

The latest rankings place India ahead of Taiwan and South Korea, restoring its position among the world's five largest equity markets.


## Three Key Drivers Behind the Rally


Lower Crude Oil Prices

A significant decline in international crude oil prices following easing geopolitical tensions in West Asia emerged as the biggest macroeconomic catalyst.

As India imports more than 80 per cent of its crude oil requirements, lower prices reduce the import bill, improve the current account balance, ease inflationary pressures and strengthen overall macroeconomic stability.

Improved Market Valuations

Following a period of correction, Indian equity valuations have become more attractive for institutional investors.

The moderation in valuation multiples, supported by continued corporate earnings growth, has improved the overall risk-reward profile of Indian equities and encouraged renewed investor participation.

Return of Foreign Institutional Investors

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs), who had shifted investments toward East Asian semiconductor markets earlier in the year, have returned as net buyers in Indian equities.

Market participants attributed the renewed inflows to easing global uncertainties, improved domestic fundamentals and policy measures aimed at facilitating foreign investment into India's capital markets.



## Global Rankings

According to recent institutional market estimates, the United States continues to lead as the world's largest equity market, followed by Mainland China in second place, Japan in third, and Hong Kong in fourth. India has now reclaimed the fifth position with a market capitalisation of $5.05 trillion. Taiwan follows in sixth place with $4.97 trillion, while South Korea stands at seventh with $4.66 trillion. India’s return to the fifth spot comes after a moderation in valuations across Taiwan and South Korea amid consolidation in their technology and semiconductor sectors.

## Outlook

Analysts believe India's diversified economic structure, robust domestic demand, expanding manufacturing base and improving macroeconomic indicators position the country favourably for sustained market growth.

While global risks related to geopolitics, commodity prices and monetary policy remain, easing crude prices, improving corporate earnings and continued foreign capital inflows are expected to provide support to Indian equities during the second half of 2026.

## At a Glance

At a GlanceIndia now holds the 5th position as the world's largest equity market with a total market capitalisation of $5.05 trillion, improving from its previous rank of 7th. The key drivers behind the rally include lower crude oil prices, renewed foreign institutional investor (FII) inflows, and more attractive valuations. Major sectors that benefited from the surge are banking, financial services, consumer goods, and manufacturing. The rally was led by benchmark indices — the BSE Sensex and NSE Nifty 50. In the process, India overtook Taiwan and South Korea. The overall outlook for Indian equities remains positive amid improving macroeconomic conditions.


## Market Perspective

India's return to the world's top five equity markets underscores the resilience of its capital markets amid global volatility. Supported by favourable macroeconomic trends, diversified sectoral growth and renewed foreign investment, the milestone reinforces India's emergence as one of the most attractive long-term investment destinations in the global financial landscape.
