LEAD: Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK) is facing a deepening political crisis as tensions escalate across major towns, including Rawalakot and Muzaffarabad. Following a series of protests spearheaded by the Joint Awami Action Committee (JAAC), the regional government has launched a significant security crackdown, invoking anti-terrorism legislation to quell civil unrest.

### The Crackdown
Activists and local organizations report an aggressive campaign by security forces to suppress dissent. The situation remains fluid, with key developments including:

*   **Mass Detentions:** More than 450 individuals have been reported arrested or detained since the unrest began six days ago.
*   **Disappearances:** The JAAC claims that several workers remain missing following demonstrations held outside the Combined Military Hospital (CMH) in Rawalakot.
*   **Communication Blackout:** Internet services, mobile networks, and other communication channels remain suspended across Muzaffarabad, Rawalakot, Poonch, Mirpur, and the Neelum Valley.
*   **Casualty Allegations:** JAAC leaders allege that the death toll from recent violence in Rawalakot has risen to 35, with numerous others injured. These figures currently await independent verification.

### Political and Social Impact

| Aspect | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Political Stance** | Supporters of the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) have amplified criticism against the security establishment's use of force. |
| **Diaspora Response** | Kashmiri groups in the UK, US, and other nations are organizing demonstrations to highlight alleged rights abuses. |
| **Historical Parallels** | Activists are drawing contentious comparisons between the current situation and the political suppression preceding the 1971 liberation of Bangladesh. |

### Global Scrutiny and Human Rights
The international community has begun to take note of the deteriorating security and human rights situation in the region. Reports indicate that international human rights organizations are actively documenting alleged civilian abuses. Furthermore, the situation is being closely monitored by foreign diplomatic missions, including the US Embassy in Pakistan.

As protests continue and competing narratives emerge, rights advocates are calling for an independent assessment of the ground reality. The primary demand remains accountability for the reported raids, arbitrary detentions, and the broader suppression of peaceful political expression in the region.
