LEAD: In what marks one of the most drastic regulatory tightening measures in recent governance history, the Central Government has systematically overhauled the framework governing how non-governmental organisations (NGOs) and civil society groups access international capital[cite: 1]. Through a sweeping gazette notification issued by the Union Home Ministry on Monday, June 22, 2026, the government has notified fundamental amendments to the Foreign Contribution Regulation Act (FCRA) Rules, 2011[cite: 1]. The new rules effectively dismantle the broad, flexible operational mandates previously enjoyed by non-profit entities, replacing them with a highly localized, rigid, and intensely audited compliance architecture[cite: 1]. Under the updated regime, NGOs can no longer secure generalized pan-India funding clearances[cite: 1]. Instead, they must map their exact geographic footprint and select narrow operational intents from a state-vetted schedule, fundamentally altering the landscape for civil society sector funding[cite: 1].

The policy shift targets three core pillars: geographical containment, ideological boundary-setting—specifically targeting religious conversion activities—and the absolute unmasking of behind-the-scenes financial beneficiaries and organizational leaders[cite: 1].
