India Dispatches Second Major Consignment of Medical Aid to Fight Ebola in Africa

43-tonne relief package containing protective equipment, diagnostics and medicines reaches Africa CDC to strengthen containment efforts in Democratic Republic of Congo.

Special Correspondent · New Delhi

In a powerful demonstration of global health solidarity, India has dispatched its second tranche of emergency medical assistance to support African nations battling a fresh Ebola outbreak.

The 43-tonne consignment, which includes personal protective equipment, advanced diagnostic tools, monitoring devices, essential medicines and nutritional supplements, was sent to the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC).

Swift Response to Emerging Crisis

The latest shipment follows the first tranche dispatched on May 29. The supplies have been routed through Africa CDC’s Eastern Regional Coordinating Centre in Uganda before being moved to the affected eastern regions of the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), where the Bundibugyo strain of Ebola has been reported.

Africa CDC expressed deep gratitude for India’s timely support, stating that the assistance will significantly bolster ongoing containment operations and help protect vulnerable communities.

India’s Growing Role in Global Health Diplomacy

External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar highlighted India’s commitment to standing with Africa during challenging times. This latest move reinforces India’s position as a reliable first responder in international public health emergencies.

Experts note that India’s rapid response not only provides immediate relief but also strengthens long-term health infrastructure across the African continent through capacity-building support.

Continued Commitment

New Delhi has reiterated that it remains in close coordination with African health authorities and is prepared to send further tranches of medical and logistical support as the situation demands. This effort underscores India’s deep-rooted philosophy of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam — the world is one family.

43 टन की राहत सामग्री में सुरक्षात्मक उपकरण, डायग्नोस्टिक और दवाएं शामिल; अफ्रीका CDC को भेजा गया।

विशेष संवाददाता · नई दिल्ली

ईबोला महामारी से जूझ रहे अफ्रीकी देशों की मदद के लिए भारत ने दूसरी बड़ी खेप चिकित्सा सहायता रवाना की है।

43 टन सामग्री में पीपीई किट, डायग्नोस्टिक उपकरण, दवाएं और पोषक सप्लीमेंट शामिल हैं।

तेज प्रतिक्रिया

पहली खेप के कुछ दिनों बाद यह दूसरी सहायता DRC के पूर्वी क्षेत्रों में पहुंचाई जा रही है। अफ्रीका CDC ने भारत का आभार व्यक्त किया है।

वैश्विक स्वास्थ्य में भारत की भूमिका

विदेश मंत्री एस जयशंकर ने इस मदद को भारत की वैश्विक जिम्मेदारी का उदाहरण बताया।
