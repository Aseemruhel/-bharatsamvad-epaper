Indian economy expected to stay resilient in FY27 despite West Asia uncertainties, says RBI

The Reserve Bank of India has projected a positive outlook for FY2026-27, stating that strong domestic demand, healthy corporate and banking sector balance sheets, continued government capital expenditure and expanding trade partnerships will help sustain growth even as geopolitical tensions in West Asia create global economic challenges.

Business Correspondent · Mumbai / New Delhi

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI), in its latest Annual Report, has expressed confidence that the Indian economy will remain one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world during FY2026-27. While acknowledging the risks arising from the ongoing conflict in West Asia, the central bank noted that India's strong macroeconomic fundamentals provide significant resilience against external shocks.

According to the report, geopolitical tensions have emerged as one of the biggest challenges for the global economy in 2026. Rising energy prices, disruptions in shipping routes and increasing uncertainty have affected global growth projections and pushed inflation expectations higher across many economies.

Growth outlook remains positive

Despite global headwinds, RBI projects India's real GDP growth at 6.9 per cent for FY2026-27. The central bank attributed this optimism to robust domestic consumption, sustained public investment, improving private sector balance sheets and strong banking sector health.

The report highlighted that continued government emphasis on infrastructure development and capital expenditure is expected to support investment activity, employment generation and long-term productivity growth.

Inflation and external risks

The RBI cautioned that prolonged geopolitical tensions could increase inflationary pressures through higher crude oil prices, commodity costs and supply-chain disruptions. However, adequate foodgrain stocks, sufficient reservoir levels and improving agricultural prospects are expected to help contain inflation within manageable levels.

Consumer price inflation for FY2026-27 is projected at 4.6 per cent, although risks remain tilted upwards because of volatile global conditions.

Exports, trade agreements and investments

While merchandise exports may face challenges due to weaker global demand and geopolitical uncertainties, India is expected to benefit from expanding trade agreements and efforts to strengthen domestic manufacturing in strategic sectors.

The RBI noted that ongoing bilateral and regional trade negotiations are likely to create new opportunities for exports, foreign investment and technology partnerships. Strong performance in software services, business services and remittance inflows is also expected to support India's external sector.

Financial system remains strong

According to the report, India's banking system remains well-capitalised and resilient. Regulatory reforms, healthy credit growth and strong balance sheets provide adequate buffers against potential external shocks.

Although global financial markets may witness volatility due to rising geopolitical risks and changing investor sentiment, the RBI stated that the domestic financial system possesses sufficient strength to absorb temporary disruptions.

The road ahead

The central bank concluded that India enters FY2026-27 from a position of relative strength. Strong private consumption, public investment, trade diversification and prudent macroeconomic management are expected to sustain growth momentum. At the same time, policymakers will continue monitoring global developments closely to ensure timely responses to emerging risks.
